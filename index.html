Animal World

Animals are living organisms that can move from one place to another, eat food, breathe, and respond to their surroundings. They cannot make their own food like plants, so they depend on plants or other animals for food.

Characteristics of Animals

  • They are living organisms
  • They move on their own
  • They breathe oxygen
  • They eat plants or other animals
  • They grow and reproduce
  • They respond to stimuli (sound, light, touch)

Types of Animals

  1. Mammals

    Mammals are animals that give birth to babies and feed milk to them.

    Most mammals have hair or fur and breathe with lungs.

    Examples: Lion, Cow, Elephant, Dog, Human

  2. Birds

    Birds have feathers, beaks, and wings.

    Most birds can fly and they lay eggs.

    Examples: Eagle, Parrot, Sparrow, Penguin, Crow

  3. Reptiles

    Reptiles have dry, scaly skin.

    They usually crawl and lay eggs.

    They are cold-blooded animals.

    Examples: Snake, Lizard, Crocodile, Turtle

  4. Amphibians

    Amphibians live both in water and on land.

    They have smooth skin and lay eggs in water.

    Examples: Frog, Toad, Salamander

  5. Fish

    Fish live only in water.

    They breathe using gills and swim using fins.

    Their body is covered with scales.

    Examples: Goldfish, Shark, Tuna

  6. Insects

    Insects have six legs and three body parts (head, thorax, abdomen).

    Most insects have wings.

    Examples: Butterfly, Ant, Bee, Mosquito

  7. Arthropod

    Arthropods have jointed legs and a hard outer body (exoskeleton).

    This group includes insects, spiders, and crabs.

    Examples: Spider, Crab, Scorpion

  8. Mollusks

    Mollusks have soft bodies.

    Some have hard shells.

    Most live in water.

    Examples: Octopus, Snail, Oyster
